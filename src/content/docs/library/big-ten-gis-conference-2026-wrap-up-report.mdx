## Executive Summary
The sixth annual Big Ten GIS Conference, held virtually on April 17, 2026, drew 340 attendees from 224 organizations — a notable 30 percent increase in attendance over 2025. Engagement held across the full day virtual program. The key takeaway is that the conference is growing where it matters: in the people who show up, stay, and contribute their own research as student presenters, reflecting the libraries’ commitment to student success.


## Wins at a Glance
- 30% increase in attendance over 2025
- All 17 GIN member universities represented among registrants
- Keynote by Frank Donnelly (Brown University) on the effort to preserve the HIFLD Open federal GIS data portal
- 67% of conference presentations were by students
- Demos of the BTAA Geoportal and new Data Citation Generator
- Session recordings posted at gin.btaa.org/conference; nearly 200 views without promotion


## 2026 Improvements
- Early faculty outreach at the start of the academic year raised the quality and competitiveness of student submissions
- Expanded proposal review: six reviewers, three per proposal, with more specific criteria than 2025
- Planning committee grew from 7 to 11 members, plus 5 dedicated day-of roles


## Looking Ahead
The 2027 planning committee will convene in December 2026. The primary operational focus: give the planning cycle more runway by opening the call for proposals in January, and convert more registrants into attendees through targeted pre-event reminders.
