      The BTAA-GIN Knowledge Committee has published the final report of the GIS
      Service Models Collaboration Group, completing a benchmarking process that
      gathered responses from 14 member institutions on how GIS services are
      structured and delivered across the network. The report documents
      consistent patterns including near-universal reliance on a single GIS
      librarian and steady growth in service demand, and identifies five areas
      for collective action:

      - Sharing service policies and user-facing documentation from mature programs for adaptation across the network

      - Exchanging knowledge about less common services and campus partnerships

      - Sharing student position descriptions and project documentation to help institutions build a case for hiring

      - Building an AI community of practice around GeoAI and generative AI

      - Developing a shared framework for evaluating and communicating the impact of GIS services
